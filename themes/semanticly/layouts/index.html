How I Work

Every decision documented

Every mapping carries its provenance. Every ontology change has an evidence trail. You'll know not just what was built, but why—and your team can maintain it without me.

Experts grow, not just their tools

Automation—including AI—can handle candidate generation, pattern matching, and bulk validation. But the goal isn't just human review of automated output. It's building your team's understanding of their own domain, so they become more capable, not more dependent.

Evidence before architecture

I start with your actual questions and the evidence that matters, not a 50-page requirements doc. The data model follows from what you need to know and what you can prove—not the other way around.

Open by default

The ODK, SSSOM, and OBO Academy all started as project-specific work that became community infrastructure. Where possible, I build things that give back. Your work benefits from shared tooling; the community benefits from yours.

Where I Think the Field Needs to Go

These are the missions I want to apply my skills to. If you're working on any of these, I'd like to talk.

Evidence-First Knowledge Systems

Structured, symbolic data is how humans encode intentions without ambiguity. I'm passionate about building knowledge systems grounded in strong evidence modelling, provenance tracking, and community deliberation—where claims are backed by transparent, auditable records.

Agentic Knowledge Graph Construction

AI agents that assemble knowledge graphs from concrete evidence records, not hallucinated triples. Combining the scalability of LLMs with the rigour of structured evidence to build graphs you can actually trust.

Environmental Data Integration

Large-scale efforts that link environmental and climate datasets to support catastrophe prevention. I'm especially interested in helping global organisations build governance structures that integrate local communities and connect their data in meaningful, lasting ways.

Strengthening Human Mental Models

Agentic systems that help humans grow, not just their tools. Projects specifically targeted at building understanding—making domain experts more capable, not more dependent on automation.

Drug Repurposing & Rare Disease

As an external consultant, I was recently embedded with Every Cure's data team, where I contributed to the design of the disease list that drives their drug repurposing candidate predictions. That experience confirmed what I already believed: the rare disease space is where rigorous data integration has the most direct human impact. I want to keep working on drug repurposing, diagnosis support, and disease data integration.

Citizen Science & Community Ontologies

Supporting citizen science communities—like building ontologies for iNaturalist enthusiasts—where passionate amateurs and domain structure meet to create something greater than the sum of its parts.
